resources #

A select few resources that I’ve found interesting or useful, and you might too.

art & design #

  • ArtAsiaPacific: periodical covering contemporary art and culture from Asia, the Pacific and the Middle East.
  • Fontshare: high-quality foundry of free fonts for both personal and commercial use.
  • Frieze: platform covering modern and contemporary art.
  • The League of Moveable Type: the world’s first open-source font foundry.

articles #

  • What can a technologist do about climate change?: an in-depth page discussing the role technologists have and their responsibility to the longevity of the planet.

creative coding & esolangs #

  • Esoteric.Codes: articles and discussion about esoteric programming languages that break from the norm of computing.
  • OpenProcessing: creative coding community.
  • Tim Rodenbroeker: helpful resource for creative coding in design.

journalism & news #

  • Associated Press: popular but balanced news wire that has over the years won 58 Pulitzer Prizes.
  • bell¿ngcat: investigative journalism group focused on human rights, war zones and the criminal world. Much of their work is focused on fact-verification and open-source intelligence.
  • Global Voices: community of writers, translators and human rights activists who reports on voiecs and experiences rarely heard in mainstream media.
  • Jacobin: admittedly biased socialist magazine that offers much-needed progressive commentary on politics, economics and culture.
  • National Public Radio (NPR): well-known broadcasting company that publishes both news and cultural programming. High quality and independent.
  • Pew Search Center: thinktank focused on informing the public about issues, attitudes and trends that are shaping the world. They do not take policy positions and are non-profit, non-partisan and non-advocacy.
  • ProPublica: not-for-profit newsroom focused on scrupulous and trustworthy investigative journalism.
  • Reuters: along with Associated Press, another popular but reliable news agency.
  • The Syllabus: a non-for-profit platform for curated content covering everything from literature to civic design. Whatever floats your boat.
  • This American Life: a weekly hour-long radio program hosted by Ira Glass, with a focus on journalistic non-fiction reporting. Highly recommend.

politics #

  • They Vote For You: Australia-focused site focusing on the actual policies your MPs vote for.

spirituality #

  • Access to Insight: helpful resource with information about Theravada Buddhism.
