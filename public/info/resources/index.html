resources #

A select few resources that I’ve found interesting or useful, and you might too.

art & design #

  • ArtAsiaPacific: periodical covering contemporary art and culture from Asia, the Pacific and the Middle East.
  • Fontshare: foundry of free fonts for both personal and commercial use.
  • Frieze: platform covering modern and contemporary art.
  • The League of Moveable Type: an open-source font foundry.

books #

  • Library Genesis: an online platform offering free access to a vast collection of books, academic papers, and other scholarly materials.

cooking #

  • Serious Eats: articles on recipes, cooking techniques, and food science.

creative coding & esolangs #

  • Esoteric.Codes: articles and discussion about esoteric programming languages that break from the norm of computing.
  • OpenProcessing: creative coding community.
  • Tim Rodenbroeker: resource for creative coding in design.

film #

  • Cahiers du Cinéma: French film magazine that gained fame for its role in the development of auteur theory and its influence on the French New Wave.
  • Cineaste: American quarterly film magazine that specializes in critical discussions, interviews, and reviews, with an emphasis on political and social themes.
  • Film Comment: American film magazine that provides insightful analysis, interviews, and reviews, with a focus on both mainstream and independent cinema from around the world.
  • Reverse Shot: online film journal that offers thoughtful criticism, essays, and analyses, often exploring the relationship between cinema and broader cultural and historical contexts.
  • Senses of Cinema: Australian online film journal that publishes in-depth articles, critical essays, and festival reports.
  • Sight & Sound: British film magazine known for its in-depth reviews, critical essays, and their prestigious “Greatest Films of All Time” poll.
  • They Shoot Pictures, Don’t They?: hosts many comprehensive lists of the best films and directors.

journalism & news #

  • Associated Press: a leading news wire service known for balanced reporting and a history of Pulitzer Prizes.
  • bell¿ngcat: investigative journalism group specializing in human rights, conflict zones, and fact-checking using open-source intelligence.
  • Global Voices: a community that highlights underrepresented voices and experiences often missing from mainstream media.
  • Jacobin: socialist magazine offering progressive commentary on politics, economics, and culture.
  • National Public Radio (NPR): major broadcaster delivering high-quality news and cultural programming with a reputation for independence.
  • Pew Search Center: non-profit think tank that provides information on social issues, trends, and attitudes without taking policy positions.
  • ProPublica: non-profit newsroom dedicated to rigorous and reliable investigative journalism.
  • Reuters: global news agency known for its trustworthy and comprehensive reporting.
  • The Syllabus: non-profit platform offering curated content on a wide range of topics, from literature to design.
  • This American Life: weekly radio program hosted by Ira Glass, with compelling non-fiction stories and journalistic reporting.

music #

  • AcclaimedMusic: a website that aggregates and compiles lists of the most critically acclaimed music albums and songs from various sources, such as critics’ polls, magazine rankings, and music publications.

politics #

  • They Vote For You: Australia-focused site focusing on the actual policies your MPs vote for.

spirituality #

  • Access to Insight: helpful resource for exploring Theravada Buddhism.

travel #

  • Atlas Obscura: a site that focuses on discovering and documenting the world’s hidden wonders, unusual places, and unique experiences.
