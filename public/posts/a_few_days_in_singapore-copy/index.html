a few days in singapore

December 24, 2024

Arriving in a another country on a day like Christmas is interesting. You’re meant to be surrounded by your family and friends, but instead you’re surrounded by a foreign culture and you’re in a world of unknowns.

That being said, Singapore was a welcome change of pace from Indonesia. The streets are clean, road rules are followed, and everything feels safe. I do have my hold-ups with Singapore though, as I found it very clinical, far too rules-based and a little lifeless.

During our first day, I became friends with one of the hostel workers, a guy called Zym, and we organised to do some karaoke in the evening. Before this, we went to one of the main streets in Singapore to see all the Christmas lights to at least embrace some level of Christmas spirit. The street was decked out in lights and it was immensely busy, and we stumbled across an Ecuadorian band playing Christmas classics. Somehow they nailed the spirit.

Afterwards, we went to karaoke, which was.. ok.. but Singapore is very expensive, and we found ourselves paying $80 for some very lacklustre karaoke and this left a bad taste in mouth. That and the bottled beer going for $15AUD a pop. ’tis the season for giving, so I guess I gave some company a lot of money to rip me off.

The next day, we managed to catch the main tourist attraction in the city, being the Gardens by the Bay. We walked through the treetops, and also visited the Cloud Forest, which is inside a giant ecosystem dome that emulates the environment found up in the mountains.

Whilst in Singapore, we also visited the Singapore Zoo (supposedly one of the best in the world). Already warmed up by the aviary back in Indonesia, we opted not to visit their bird sanctuary, and just see the classic zoo fare - giraffes, lions, tigers, you get it.

The general area we stayed in, on Joo Chiat Road, is in the old town of Singapore and has a lot of pretty buildings with European-esque architecture and pastel colours.

We only needed a few days in Singapore as it is really just a singular city, and with a heavy focus on shopping and finance which isn’t really our jam. We were headed to Hong Kong and made sure to arrive at Singapore Airport early as it is an attraction in itself. It had an indoors circular waterfall which was quite impressive, as well as a butterfly enclosure, although we did not see the latter as we had a plane to catch.
